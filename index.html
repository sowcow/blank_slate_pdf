E.pdf (/ɛ/)

It's the latest minimalistic and abstract PDF.
The general idea is that the PDF provides ways to store records in different modes of organization,
while the exact scenarios for its use are unconstrained and are up to the user.
For that reason all pages have common header for switching between different modes.

- .: scratchpad pages, can be used for temporary content; use it by entering a random link inside the circle
- -: can be a catchall for unorganized items; it is a sequence of multiple pages (more organized items may go into squares)
- +: organization by days: 36 linked pages in a calendar-like format, configurable, predefined columns of text on every page (the default example configuration uses numbers at the right for time, and three categories at the left, this can be changed/removed in configuration text fields below the button "Make E")
- ▲: organization by time period divided with checkpoints; use it by entering a link with the needed starting time and period
- ■: organization by topic/event(s); it can be seen as separate habit calendars, each having a linkink to a sequence of content pages from its header
- the circle is simply a circle

Header of every page can be set to the month name.
Also, the `+` grid is configurable so it may have experimental non-calendar uses.

Teeth.pdf



      Teeth.pdf is inspired by After Hours and has a room for The Shining too.
      Main "+" view is a monthly grid with further daily todo lists
      (can be Teeth or Sandworm version or can have both depending on selected options).
      The "#" view is a maze - place for catchall pages for unrelated ideas,
      they can be stored in further Δ pages.
      The "-" view starts from the first page of the PDF,
      it is for higher level content like conception of usage of the PDF,
      or possibly for review-type notes accumulation, it's the only multi-page entry.

      Below are the settings for Make Teeth;
      there is an option to have cryptic review pages (Space Odyssey inspired);

      Most recent and new default option is to use Sandworm todo list:
      If "+" page is used like a monthly calendar then you have one Sandworm per day.
      Inside of the Sandworm you have very limited space for todo list, day focus or similar content.
      Outside of it there is clockface with 12 squares to do time planning or accounting.
      Whether to slay the Sandworm at the end of the day or to feed maidens into it is the discretion of the user.

      I have gray colored lines by default for Remarkable2,
      use any hex color code if RM-PRO is the target device,
      like orange is used for other PDFs below.

      If multiple options are selected then they are accessible by turning the page.
